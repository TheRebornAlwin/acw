# WWP Strategy Brief: AC Windows & Cleaning Services

## QUESTION 1: Business Objective
Build a high-converting single-page website that positions AC Windows & Cleaning Services as Barry's most reliable and trusted exterior cleaning service. The primary conversion goal is driving phone calls for free quotes.

### Funnel Piece Needed
A landing page / homepage that takes a visitor from "I need my exterior cleaned" to "I'm calling this company right now." The page must move them through awareness levels 2-3 to level 4 (ready to act).

### Who Am I Talking To
**Primary:** Women aged 35-55 who manage household decisions. NHS workers, teachers, civil servants, solicitors — dual-income professionals commuting from Barry to Cardiff. Homeowners in semi-detached and detached houses in the Vale of Glamorgan (71.9% homeowner rate). Combined household income ~55-75k. They are time-poor, house-proud, and have been burned by unreliable cleaners before.

**Secondary:** Elderly homeowners (55-75) who physically cannot clean upstairs windows, gutters, or driveways.

---

## QUESTION 2: Where Are They Now?

### Current State
- Dirty, grimy windows that block light and look embarrassing
- Mossy, slippery driveways that are a safety hazard and an eyesore
- Blocked gutters overflowing every time it rains, causing damp anxiety
- Moss-damaged roof tiles they're worried about
- Exhausted from searching for reliable cleaners — most no-show or do shoddy work
- Tried DIY pressure washing, took all day, damaged the surface, "never again"
- Feel ashamed when visitors arrive or neighbours walk past
- Afraid of hidden damage (damp, mould, insurance implications)

### Dream State
- A home that looks "like new" from the street
- Sparkling windows that flood rooms with light
- A driveway you're proud of — clean, bright, slip-free
- Gutters flowing freely, no more overflow anxiety
- A reliable service that just happens without chasing or worrying
- Being "that house" on the street — the one that looks immaculate
- Pride, relief, one less thing on the mental load

### Market Awareness Level: 2-3
- Level 2: They know they have a problem (dirty exterior)
- Level 3: They know solutions exist (cleaning services)
- They do NOT know AC Windows specifically (Level 4 not reached)
- **Strategy:** Call out the problem briefly, introduce AC Windows as the best solution, then drive to action with proof and trust

### Market Sophistication: Stage 2-3
- Most local competitors are at Stage 2 (bigger claims: "premier," "specialist," "best")
- Leading-edge competitors moving to Stage 3 (mechanisms: water-fed poles, soft washing, named processes)
- **Strategy:** Lead with a unique mechanism (visual accountability through before/after documentation, reliability guarantee) to enter Stage 3 before local competitors
- **Key insight:** No local competitor uses video content, before/after sliders, or founder storytelling. These are massive differentiation opportunities

### Desire/Certainty/Trust Levels
| Factor | Current | Required | Gap |
|--------|---------|----------|-----|
| Desire | 6/10 | 8/10 | +2 |
| Certainty | 3/10 | 7/10 | +4 |
| Trust | 2/10 | 7/10 | +5 |

**Biggest gap = Trust (+5).** They don't know us. We must build trust aggressively through owner photo, named team, real work photos, reviews, guarantee, local credentials.

**Second gap = Certainty (+4).** They've been burned before. We must show proof that our approach works: before/after visuals, reviews mentioning reliability, clear process explanation.

## QUESTION 3: Where Do We Want Them?

### Desired Action
Call the phone number for a free, no-obligation quote.

### Perceived Costs to Address
- **Fear of commitment:** "What if I call and they pressure me?" → Frame as "no obligation, no pressure"
- **Fear of being let down again:** "Every window cleaner disappears eventually" → Address with reliability messaging + guarantee
- **Time to call:** "I'll do it later" → Create urgency subtly (seasonal, limited slots)
- **Price uncertainty:** "I don't know what this costs" → "Free quote" removes financial risk

### Top Objections
1. "Will they actually show up?" → Reliability guarantee + reviews
2. "How much will it cost?" → Free quote, no obligation
3. "Are they any good?" → Before/after photos, reviews, guarantee
4. "Are they insured?" → State clearly, name insurer
5. "I can do it myself" → Show DIY vs professional difference
6. "I don't have time to arrange this" → Easy booking, flexible scheduling

## QUESTION 4: The Journey (Page Structure)

### Step-by-step psychological progression:

1. **HOOK (Hero)** — Grab attention with dramatic before/after video transformation. Headline addresses the outcome, not the service. CTA immediately visible.

2. **BUILD DESIRE (Trust Bar + Services)** — Quick credibility strip (stats, insurance, rating). Then show ALL services with benefit-focused copy and real images. Paint the dream state.

3. **BUILD CERTAINTY (Gallery + How It Works)** — Overwhelming visual proof via before/after gallery. Then explain the simple process to reduce anxiety. Show them exactly what to expect.

4. **BUILD TRUST (Reviews + About)** — Social proof from real customers (or realistic placeholders). Then the founder story — Anthony's face, his story, his commitment. Personal connection.

5. **HANDLE OBJECTIONS (FAQ)** — Address every hesitation directly. Cost, reliability, insurance, weather, payment methods.

6. **DRIVE ACTION (Final CTA)** — Strong emotional close. Remind them of the pain of inaction. Make calling feel easy and risk-free.

## Core Messaging Hierarchy

1. **Primary message:** "Reliable. Every time." — The #1 differentiator in a market plagued by no-shows
2. **Secondary message:** Visual transformation — "Same house. Completely different."
3. **Tertiary message:** Local, personal, trustworthy — "Your neighbour, not a stranger"

## Copy Voice
- Speak like a mate, not a corporation
- Short, punchy sentences mixed with longer flowing ones
- Sensory language: sparkling, gleaming, fresh, bright, spotless
- Mirror the verbatim language from the research
- Never say "leverage," "solutions," "utilize," or any AI cliché
- Address the reader directly: "your home," "you deserve"
