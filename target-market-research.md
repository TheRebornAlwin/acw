# Target Market Research: AC Windows & Cleaning Services
## Barry, Vale of Glamorgan & Cardiff, Wales

## 1. DEMOGRAPHICS

### Who Typically Hires These Services? Men or Women?

Women are the primary decision-makers for hiring domestic cleaning services in the UK. According to Statista, women are disproportionately responsible for household cleaning decisions. On Mumsnet (the UK's largest parenting forum, overwhelmingly female), window cleaner discussions are extremely active, with women arranging, evaluating, and firing window cleaners. However, the person who answers the door or pays may be either gender. In practice, it is usually the woman in the household who researches, books, and manages ongoing relationships with cleaning service providers. For marketing purposes, your primary audience is women aged 30-55 who manage the household.

### Approximate Age Range

- **Primary target: 35-55 years old.** This is the age group most likely to be homeowners with disposable income who value a well-maintained home but lack the time or physical ability to do it themselves.
- **Secondary target: 55-75 years old.** Elderly homeowners who physically cannot clean gutters, reach upstairs windows, or operate pressure washers. The median age in the Vale of Glamorgan is 44 years old (higher than the Wales average of 42).
- **Emerging target: 25-34 years old.** Millennials are a growing market -- 40% of people aged 25-34 in the UK either already hire a cleaner or are actively looking for one.

### Occupation Types

Cardiff and the Vale of Glamorgan have a professional, white-collar workforce:

- **Public administration, education, and health** -- 32% of Cardiff's workforce (NHS workers, teachers, council employees, university staff)
- **Financial and business services** -- 20% of Cardiff's workforce (accountants, solicitors, insurance, banking)
- **Distribution, hotels, and restaurants** -- 20.4%
- **49% of jobs in the Vale of Glamorgan are at professional level**, with the majority holding degree-equivalent qualifications
- **40% of Cardiff's workforce are daily commuters** from surrounding areas including Barry and the Vale -- these are time-poor professionals who leave early and get home late
- Many Vale of Glamorgan residents commute to Cardiff for work (approximately 70,900 people commute into Cardiff daily)

**Typical occupations of your customers:** NHS nurses and doctors, schoolteachers, civil servants, solicitors, accountants, small business owners, retired professionals, and dual-income families where both partners work full-time.

### Income Levels in Barry and Cardiff Area

- **Average salary in Cardiff:** approximately 41,100-43,100 per year (2024-2025 data)
- **Average salary in Vale of Glamorgan:** approximately 37,300-38,000 per year
- **UK average for comparison:** 45,800 (2024) to 48,500 (2025)
- **Typical household income** (dual earners): 55,000-75,000 for the target demographic
- **Average house price in Vale of Glamorgan:** 296,000 (November 2025)
- **Average first-time buyer price:** 236,000
- **Average home-mover price:** 361,000

These are solidly middle-income areas. Not wealthy enough to have full-time household staff, but comfortable enough to pay 20-40 per month for window cleaning and periodic pressure washing/gutter cleaning. Your ideal customer earns enough to value their time more than the cost of your service.

### Housing Types

Barry and the Vale of Glamorgan have a diverse housing stock:

- **Semi-detached houses** -- the most common property type across Barry and the wider Vale
- **Terraced houses** -- very common in Barry town centre and the older parts of Barry (particularly Victorian and Edwardian terraces)
- **Detached houses** -- prevalent in more affluent areas of the Vale (Cowbridge, Llantwit Major, Rhoose, Sully, and parts of Penarth)
- **Flats/apartments** -- found in Barry town centre and newer developments near Barry waterfront/Barry Island
- **The Vale of Glamorgan has the highest proportion of households with 4+ bedrooms** in Wales at 28.2% (16,000 homes) -- this indicates larger, more affluent properties that need more cleaning

Cardiff has more terraced housing and flats, particularly in areas like Canton, Cathays, Roath, Splott, and Grangetown. The suburbs (Whitchurch, Rhiwbina, Llandaff, Cyncoed, Lisvane) have more semi-detached and detached properties.

### Homeowners vs Renters

**Vale of Glamorgan:**
- **71.9% homeowners** -- joint highest in Wales
- 15.6% private renters (up from 14.6% in 2011)
- Remainder in social housing

**Cardiff:**
- **58.3% homeowners** -- the lowest in Wales
- 24.3% private renters (highest in Wales)
- Remainder in social housing

**For your business:** The Vale of Glamorgan is your strongest market for regular domestic customers because nearly 3 in 4 households are owner-occupiers who have a direct financial and emotional investment in maintaining their property. In Cardiff, focus on suburban homeowners rather than city-centre renters. Landlords with rental portfolios are also a viable commercial target.

## 2. PAINFUL CURRENT STATE

### What Are They Afraid Of?

**Regarding dirty windows:**
- Light not getting into the house, making rooms feel dark and gloomy
- Visitors noticing the grime, especially on sunny days when every smear shows
- The house looking uncared-for from the street

**Regarding blocked gutters:**
- **Water damage and damp** -- this is the number one fear. Overflowing gutters cause penetrating damp, mould growth, saturated brickwork, and in extreme cases, ceiling collapse. One Mumsnet user posted: "I've now come downstairs this morning to the ceiling caved in." Another wrote about discovering "brickwork was completely saturated where the gutter had been leaking."
- **Expensive repair bills** -- gutter-related damp repairs can cost 500-1,200+. One homeowner was quoted 1,200 by a roofer for damage that started with a simple blockage
- **Insurance implications** -- UK insurers can deny claims if neglect is proven. Moss-covered roofs and blocked gutters may void home insurance claims

**Regarding dirty driveways/patios:**
- Slipping on moss and algae (genuine safety hazard, particularly for elderly homeowners and children)
- The surface deteriorating permanently if moss is left to take hold
- Reduced property value -- estate agents estimate kerb appeal impacts house value by 5-10%, and sellers with poor kerb appeal receive on average 57,000 less than asking price

**Regarding dirty roofs:**
- Moss damaging tiles, causing leaks during freeze-thaw cycles
- Insurance policy cancellation or premium increases
- The house looking old and neglected from the street

### What Frustrates Them About Their Current Situation?

Based on extensive forum research, the top frustrations are:

1. **Unreliable window cleaners.** This is by far the biggest pain point. Forum after forum reveals the same story: "I've asked 4 to come in the last 6 months, 2 just never turned up." Another: "Every 8 weeks, love my window cleaner, but before him we spent 15 years struggling to get a reliable service."

2. **Not knowing when the cleaner will show up.** "I just hate not knowing when they are going to turn up." And: "Random days too."

3. **Poor quality work.** "He never seemed to do the corners." "They say they'll clean the back windows... they never do it." "One guy called to collect money, saying his mate cleaned them... windows were filthy."

4. **Being charged for work not done.** "Over the course of ten years two have vanished after being paid in advance." "Swore blind a few weeks ago that we owed for 2 cleans and not one."

5. **Aggressive or pushy behaviour.** "He demanded payment." "He was very rude to me last time." "He got agro when I asked him to talk slower."

6. **DIY pressure washing is exhausting and risky.** Homeowners who try DIY driveway cleaning report it takes hours, damages surfaces when done wrong, and many say "never again."

7. **Not being able to clean upstairs windows safely.** Ladders are dangerous for untrained people, and most homeowners do not own water-fed poles.

### What Are They Embarrassed About?

- Windows so dirty you can see the grime from the street
- Green/black algae streaks running down the front of the house
- A mossy, stained driveway that looks worse than every other house on the street
- Inviting people over and hoping they do not notice the filthy conservatory roof or green-tinged windows
- The contrast when one neighbour's house looks immaculate and theirs looks neglected
- Having "that house" on the street -- the one that brings down the tone

### How Does a Dirty Exterior Make Them Feel About Their Home?

- **Ashamed** -- they know what it should look like but feel powerless to fix it
- **Anxious** -- especially about hidden damage (damp, roof problems)
- **Overwhelmed** -- the longer they leave it, the worse it gets, and the bigger the job seems
- **Defeated** -- they have tried to find reliable help and been let down repeatedly
- **House-poor** -- they own a nice home but it does not look like it from the outside

### What Do Neighbours/Visitors Think?

- "They must not take care of their house"
- "I wonder what the inside looks like if the outside is this bad"
- "That house brings down the whole street"
- Buyers on Google Earth can see the state of the neighbourhood -- dirty/neglected homes affect nearby property values
- As one Mumsnet user noted, neighbours are known for "staring and analysing our house"

### If They Were Describing the Problem to a Friend Over Dinner, What Would They Say?

Based on actual forum language, here is how they talk about it:

- "Honestly, I can't find a decent window cleaner to save my life. The last one just stopped turning up and I've been ringing around for months."
- "My gutters are overflowing every time it rains and I'm terrified it's going to cause damp. I just don't know who to call."
- "The driveway is an absolute state. It's covered in moss and it looks like nobody lives here. I keep meaning to get someone in but I never get round to it."
- "I bought one of those Karcher things from Aldi but honestly it took me all day and I nearly wrecked the paving. Never again."
- "Every time someone comes to the door I'm mortified. The front of the house looks awful."
- "We've been in the house over a year and I still haven't found a reliable window cleaner. They all seem to have gravely ill family members, broken vans, broken equipment or a lack of communication device."
- "I did complain after the last wash as the windows were still dirty but they came out and redid them -- shouldn't have to ask though, should I?"

### What's Keeping Them from Solving the Problem Now?

1. **They do not know who to trust.** Previous bad experiences have made them wary.
2. **They do not have time to research and vet services.** Busy professionals and working parents.
3. **They are worried about cost.** They do not know what a fair price is (window cleaning ranges from 7-30/month locally; pressure washing averages 250 for a driveway).
4. **They tried DIY and it did not work** -- or it was too time-consuming and physically demanding.
5. **They are afraid of being ripped off** -- especially by door-to-door gutter cleaners who upsell unnecessary work.
6. **They keep putting it off** -- it feels like a low-priority task until it becomes an emergency (collapsed ceiling, insurance issue, house sale).
7. **Safety concerns** -- they cannot safely clean upstairs windows or gutters themselves.

### What Would Their Ideal Home Exterior Look Like?

- Sparkling, streak-free windows that let the light flood in
- Clean, bright driveway and patio that look freshly laid
- Gutters flowing freely with no moss, leaves, or overflow
- A roof that looks clean and well-maintained
- The whole house looking fresh, cared-for, and welcoming
- "It looks like new" -- this exact phrase appears repeatedly in customer reviews

### Who Do They Want to Impress?

1. **Themselves** -- they want to feel proud when they pull up to their own home
2. **Their neighbours** -- they want to be the house that looks good, not the one that lets the street down
3. **Visitors and family** -- especially when hosting (Christmas, barbecues, children's parties)
4. **Potential buyers** -- if they are thinking of selling, kerb appeal is top of mind
5. **Their partner** -- showing they take care of the home and are on top of things

### How Would They Feel with Sparkling Windows and Clean Surfaces?

Based on actual customer review language:
- "I always notice my clean windows... and doors!" -- a sense of genuine pleasure and noticing the difference daily
- Relief and "peace of mind" that the house is maintained
- Pride -- "taking pride in your home boosts your self-esteem"
- A weight lifted -- one less thing to worry about
- Satisfaction at the transformation -- "couldn't believe what was underneath it!"
- Confidence when people visit or drive past

- **Someone they can trust completely** -- who shows up when they say they will, does a proper job, and charges fairly.
- **The feeling of a "new" house** without the cost of actually renovating.

## 4. VALUES, BELIEFS & TRIBAL AFFILIATIONS

### What Do They Believe About DIY vs Hiring Professionals?

- Most have tried DIY at some point. Common pattern: they bought a cheap pressure washer, spent an entire weekend on the driveway, got mediocre results, and damaged something. "Never again."
- They believe upstairs windows and gutters are genuinely dangerous to DIY -- and they are right.
- They believe professionals have better equipment and get better results faster. One forum comment noted a DIY driveway clean takes 1-2 hours vs 30-60 minutes for a professional.
- They believe hiring a professional is "worth it" but only if the professional is actually reliable and competent -- which many have not been.
- There is a cultural element in Wales and the UK where people feel slightly guilty about "paying someone to clean your windows" (as if it is a luxury), but they quickly get over it when they experience the convenience.

### What Have They Tried Before That Did Not Work?

1. **Unreliable window cleaners** -- the number one failed experience. Cleaners who disappear, skip visits, demand cash, do poor work, or turn up unannounced.
2. **DIY pressure washing** -- too time-consuming, risk of surface damage, mediocre results.
3. **Cheap consumer pressure washers** (Karcher, Aldi specials) -- not powerful enough for serious moss/algae removal.
4. **Ignoring the problem** -- hoping it will sort itself out. It never does; it only gets worse.
5. **Door-to-door gutter cleaners** -- mixed experiences, with many reports of scams and upselling on forums like MoneySavingExpert.
6. **Asking a "man with a van" or handyman** -- inconsistent quality and no ongoing relationship.

### How Do They Evaluate Cleaning Services?

Based on forum and review analysis, customers evaluate services on (in order of priority):

1. **Reliability** -- Do they show up when they say they will? This is far and away the number one criterion.
2. **Communication** -- Do they let you know when they are coming? Do they text ahead? Do they respond to messages?
3. **Quality of work** -- Are the windows actually clean? Did they do the backs as well as the fronts? Did they do the frames and sills?
4. **Price/value** -- Not necessarily the cheapest, but fair. Typical local prices: 12-30/month for window cleaning.
5. **Professionalism** -- Are they polite? Do they clean up after themselves? Are they insured?
6. **Reviews and recommendations** -- Word of mouth is king. Mumsnet users overwhelmingly say "ask around" for recommendations. Google reviews and Facebook group recommendations carry significant weight.
7. **Easy payment** -- Direct debit and bank transfer preferred. Many customers are uncomfortable with cash-only operators.

### What Character Traits Do They Value in Tradespeople?

- **Reliable** -- turns up when promised, every time
- **Honest** -- charges fairly, does not upsell unnecessary work, admits if something is not right
- **Friendly and polite** -- "Ben and Brian are friendly, helpful, polite and most importantly do a great job!"
- **Professional** -- insured, uniformed, uses proper equipment, leaves no mess
- **Communicative** -- texts before arrival, sends reminders, responds to queries
- **Thorough** -- does the job properly without cutting corners; "attention to detail was very thorough"
- **Trustworthy** -- can be around the property without supervision
- **Local and established** -- they prefer someone who lives in the community and has a reputation to protect

### What Traits Do They Despise?

- **Unreliability** -- the number one complaint across every forum. Not showing up is unforgivable.
- **Demanding cash and being aggressive about payment** -- "He demanded payment." "He said he'd rather have cash than a bank transfer."
- **Turning up unannounced** and cleaning without permission, then demanding money -- "randomly coming to clean someone's windows without knocking first... is not okay"
- **Dishonesty** -- claiming to have cleaned when they clearly have not; "one guy called to collect money, saying his mate cleaned them... windows were filthy"
- **Poor communication** -- not answering calls, not giving advance notice
- **Being pushy or rude** -- "He got agro when I asked him to talk slower"
- **Door-to-door cold calling with pressure tactics** -- especially gutter cleaners who "find" extra problems
- **Disappearing after being paid in advance**

### What Trends Are They Aware Of?

- **Eco-friendly cleaning:** 60% of UK consumers are willing to pay a premium for environmentally friendly cleaning alternatives. Green cleaning certifications are seen as a mark of trust and quality.
- **Water-fed pole technology:** Many customers now expect this rather than traditional ladder-and-bucket methods. It is faster, safer, and gives better results.
- **Pure water systems:** Customers are increasingly aware that purified water leaves no streaks or residue.
- **Online booking and scheduling:** Customers expect to be able to book, pay, and manage their service digitally.
- **Regular maintenance packages:** Customers prefer monthly subscriptions over one-off bookings -- it is easier and ensures the house stays consistently clean.
- **Value for money is the dominant 2026 trend:** Over 40% of cleaning industry respondents identified "demonstrating value for money" as the biggest trend for 2026.

## 7. SUMMARY OF KEY MARKETING INSIGHTS

### The Number One Message That Will Resonate:

**"Reliable. Every time."**

Every piece of research points to the same conclusion: the market is not suffering from a lack of window cleaners. It is suffering from a lack of *reliable* window cleaners. If AC Windows & Cleaning Services can become known as the company that always shows up, always communicates, and always does a thorough job, it will dominate the local market.

### Secondary Messages That Will Resonate:

1. **"We text you the day before we come."** -- Addresses the "never knowing when they'll turn up" frustration.
2. **"Your windows, gutters, and driveway -- all sorted."** -- The convenience of one company for all exterior cleaning.
3. **"Sparkling windows, no hassle, fair price."** -- Uses the exact language customers use in positive reviews.
4. **"Like new."** -- The two most powerful words in every positive exterior cleaning review.
5. **"Protect your home from hidden damage."** -- Appeals to the fear of damp, mould, and insurance issues from neglected gutters and roofs.

### Price Benchmarks (Local Market):

- **Window cleaning (residential):** 12-30 per visit, typically 4-weekly
- **Gutter cleaning:** 60-240 depending on property size and condition
- **Driveway pressure washing:** 150-350 depending on size (average UK is 250)
- **Roof cleaning (soft wash):** 300-800 depending on size
- **Conservatory roof cleaning:** 50-150

### Competitive Advantage Opportunities:

1. Offer monthly subscription packages (windows + gutters + annual driveway wash)
2. Text/WhatsApp communication before every visit
3. Before/after photos sent to the customer after every pressure washing or gutter clean
4. Direct debit payment (removes the awkward cash exchange)
5. Satisfaction guarantee -- return free of charge if not happy (Through The Seasons in the Vale already offers this)
6. Eco-friendly cleaning products (60% of UK consumers will pay more for this)
7. Consistent, professional branding across van, uniform, flyers, and social media
